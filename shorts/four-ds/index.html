Ds for working well
with Claude.

A practice loop. Scroll through to meet each one.

D
No. 01

Delegation.

The first decision is whether AI should touch this at all.

Pick the right task and the right model. Some work is yours alone. Some belongs to a teammate over coffee. Some is a perfect fit for Haiku, some needs Opus, some needs a subagent so the main thread stays clean.

What is this task actually asking for, and who or what is best suited to it?
D
No. 02

Description.

Tell Claude what a smart colleague would need.

The goal. The context already in your head. The constraints. The success criteria. The format. Specs reduce ambiguity. Vagueness is expensive, and you pay for it in revisions.

Did I give it enough to make a real judgment call, or am I hoping it guesses right?
D
No. 03

Discernment.

Three lenses, every time.

  • ProductIs the answer correct?
  • ProcessDid the reasoning hold up?
  • PerformanceWas it communicated clearly?
Am I trusting this because it sounds confident, or because I checked?
D
No. 04

Diligence.

Verify, attribute, own it.

You are accountable for whatever ships under your name. Test the code. Read the cited source. Run the migration on a copy. The AI did not make the decision. You did.

Would I stake my reputation on this exact output, exactly as it stands?
The shape of the work

It is a loop, not a line.

Discernment fails, you sharpen the description. Diligence catches a gap, you delegate differently next time. Each pass through the loop is the practice.

Four Ds. One loop. A working life.

Nobody nails this on the first prompt. The fluency is built in the loop, every time you delegate, describe, discern, and own the result.

Run it again tomorrow. Run it again next week. The loop is the practice, and the practice is the skill.
